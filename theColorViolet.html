About

Tory Lanez's 'The Color Violet' is a poignant exploration of love, heartbreak, and the internal conflict of a playboy persona. The song delves into the complexities of a romantic encounter that is both thrilling and destructive. Lanez's lyrics paint a vivid picture of a fleeting relationship, marked by a fast-paced lifestyle and the allure of materialism. The mention of a 'Barbie' and the 'X on the dot' suggests a superficial connection, one that is perhaps more about image and instant gratification than genuine affection. The protagonist's admission of being a loner, despite the woman's interest in his 'watch,' 'drop-top,' and 'persona,' indicates a guarded nature and a reluctance to fully engage emotionally.

The Color Violet is the 4th song to appear on Tory's “Alone At Prom” album. Tory sings about heartbreak and tells us a story about a woman whom he lusts for but she’s appears to be no good for him. She eventually breaks his heart while “playing games of love” to deceive Tory. Lanez doesn’t think he can recover from this hence the chorus “I won't dance again”

Q&A

Who wrote “The Color Violet” by Tory Lanez?

“The Color Violet” by Tory Lanez was written by Nik Dean, Foreign Teck, Tory Lanez & Roark Bailey.

What certifications has this track received?

On May 31, 2023, “The Color Violet” was certified platinum by the RIAA.
