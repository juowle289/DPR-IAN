MY FAVORITE SINGER
SONGS

Violet Crazy

The song delves into the intricate dynamics of a tumultuous and intense relationship. The lyrics depict a sense of being consumed by the allure and unpredictability of a partner, described as "violet crazy." The singer grapples with the conflicting emotions of being drawn to this wild and passionate connection while also feeling tired and uncertain about its longevity. There is a theme of escapism and a desire for freedom from the overwhelming nature of the relationship, as hinted by the plea to "set me free." Despite the challenges and emotional exhaustion, the singer is captivated by the fairy tale-like quality of their bond, willing to hold onto it until they have to say goodbye. "Violet Crazy" ultimately explores the bittersweet nature of love, with its intoxicating highs and burdensome lows, leaving a lingering sense of ambiguity and longing for clarity.

Don't Go Insane

The protagonist struggles with the idea of manic alter egos represented by wolves that seem to be coming for them, symbolizing a looming loss of control. The lyrics reflect a plea for acceptance and understanding from others, as well as a sense of isolation and despair. The repetition of "insane" throughout the song hints at the protagonist's inner turmoil and fear of their struggles going unnoticed or being dismissed. Ultimately, the track serves as a poignant exploration of mental health struggles and the desperate attempt to cling to one's sense of self amid chaos and uncertainty.

Nerves

Regret and being scared to move forward in a relationship. It tells the story of someone who has made a mistake in leaving someone they cared about, and who is now feeling scared of getting hurt again. The narrator is expressing the fear of opening up again and the regret of not holding on to the person they left. The song also speaks to the other person explaining the narrator's feelings while apologising for the hurt they caused.

So Beautiful

“So Beautiful” depicts Mito in his darkest form as a misconceived monster much like Frankenstein, inspired by the idea that something so “dark and sinister is actually something that’s … the most misunderstood.” It’s about inviting one to find beauty in the imperfect human condition and embrace their inner demons: “Let's take a trip down a dark place, baby / Look for me now I'm not that (crazy).”

Peanut Butter & Tears

The word "Peanut Butter" can symbolize moments of joy and comfort, while "Tears" represents sadness and tears. Captures a mix of bittersweet emotions, reflecting the singer's inner turmoil and the impact on their relationships. As the song progresses, there is a theme of embracing one's true self and finding liberation through self-acceptance

Scaredy Cat

Repeating the phrase "I'm a scaredy cat" to emphasize the feeling of being constantly scared and on edge. The song is an emotional plea to be heard and understood, and to be shown compassion and support. It calls for understanding and courage in the face of a seemingly insurmountable fear, and shines a light on the reality of feeling alone and scared while longing for connection and understanding.
